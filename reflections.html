Reflections

General Education Reflection

Across humanities, social sciences, and technical electives, I learned how context shapes engineering choices. Courses in communication and culture improved how I write specs and present trade‑offs, while statistics courses sharpened my ability to reason about uncertainty and measurement error.

Cumulative Reflection

My engineering path has been about turning ideas into reliable systems. Android and web projects taught me to define clear APIs and keep state predictable. Systems labs in C taught me to respect concurrency and test edge cases early. Group projects reinforced that communication avoids hours of debugging later. I now approach problems with small testable slices, observability, and an eye toward maintainability and ethics.

Ethics Paper (CPRE/EE 394)

Abstract: This paper evaluates the privacy implications of location tracking apps in education and family contexts.
